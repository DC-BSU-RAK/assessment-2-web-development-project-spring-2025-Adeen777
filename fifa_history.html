The Evolution of FIFA

FIFA video game series is the best-selling and longest-running sports game series of all time. Here is the history behind this legendary sport:

1. The Beginning (1993–1999):
The franchise started with FIFA International Soccer in December 1993. It featured real national teams and annual releases improved graphics and gameplay over time.

2. The Rise (2000–2009):
FIFA 2001 onwards brought real player data and club licenses. FIFA 2003 enhanced realism significantly. FIFA 09 introduced refined controls and licensed leagues.

3. The Peak (2010–2016):
Games like FIFA 10 to 14 brought Virtual Pro, Career Mode upgrades, Impact Engine, and Tactical Defending. Ultimate Team mode gained massive popularity during this period.

4. The Peak Era (2016–2022):
FIFA 17 introduced the Frostbite Engine and "The Journey" story mode. Women's football and online modes like FUT Champions gained prominence.

5. End of FIFA Partnership (2022):
FIFA 23 was the final game under the FIFA name. EA and FIFA parted ways over licensing issues. EA retained player, club, and league rights separately.

6. EA Sports FC Era (2023–Present):
EA rebranded to EA Sports FC starting with EA Sports FC 24 in 2023. The game continued improving realism and gameplay in FC 25 with deeper tactical features.
